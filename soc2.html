What digger contributes to a SOC 2

SOC 2 reports (Type I or Type II) attest to the design — and for Type II, the operating effectiveness over a 3-12 month window — of a service organization's controls against the AICPA Trust Services Criteria. The Security category (the Common Criteria, CC1-CC9) is always in scope; the Additional Criteria (Availability, Confidentiality, Processing Integrity, Privacy) are included when the engagement scope requires them.

digger isn't a SOC 2 GRC platform. It's the technical evidence half: an auditable, hash-chained record of the endpoint controls actually operating during the audit window. Combined with your policy documentation, training records, and ticketing data, digger output forms defensible evidence for a meaningful subset of TSC criteria.

A defensible Type II evidence workflow

For a 6-month Type II window, digger should run on every in-scope endpoint on a fixed schedule — typically weekly or bi-weekly. Each run contributes one PQC-signed case directory to the audit evidence repository:

#!/bin/bash
# /usr/local/bin/digger-soc2-run.sh
set -euo pipefail

CASE_DIR="/var/lib/digger/soc2/$(date -u +%Y-%m-%dT%H%M%SZ)"
KEY=/var/lib/digger/keys/soc2.sk

digger --no-banner \
       --classification "Confidential" \
       --tlp TLP:AMBER+STRICT \
       investigate --case-dir "$CASE_DIR" \
       --report "$CASE_DIR/report.html"

digger --no-banner compliance assess \
       --case-dir "$CASE_DIR" \
       --frameworks soc2_tsc

digger --no-banner pqc sign \
       --case-dir "$CASE_DIR" \
       --algorithm ML-DSA-65 \
       --key "$KEY" \
       --note "SOC 2 Type II evidence run, host=$(hostname)"

# upload to your immutable evidence store (S3 Object Lock, etc.)
aws s3 sync "$CASE_DIR" "s3://soc2-evidence/$(hostname)/$(basename "$CASE_DIR")/" \
    --storage-class GLACIER_IR

Schedule via systemd timer or cron. Verify the chain at audit time:

digger verify --case-dir /var/lib/digger/soc2/2026-03-15T120000Z
digger pqc verify --case-dir /var/lib/digger/soc2/2026-03-15T120000Z

What the auditor will ask for

Sampling
Auditors test a sample of dates across the window. Run digger at least weekly so any chosen sample date has corresponding evidence.
Tamper resistance
The hash chain (digger verify) plus a PQC signature (digger pqc sign) demonstrates the evidence wasn't modified after collection. Store the public key separately from the case directories.
Sequence of events
The chain_of_custody.json sidecar records every operation. It's a per-case audit log distinct from the evidence itself, satisfying the "auditability" requirement (ISO 27037 §6.3) that SOC 2 auditors increasingly look for.
Coverage assertion
The compliance assessor report lists every TSC control and the specific check that produced its status. Hand this directly to the auditor as evidence of automated coverage; supply your policy docs for the manual ones.

Cross-framework mapping

The SOC 2 catalog includes mappings to NIST 800-53, ISO 27001:2022, and PCI DSS 4.0. If your organization also has a NIST 800-171 / CMMC or ISO 27001 program, digger can produce all of those reports off the same evidence:

digger compliance assess --case-dir ./case-1 \
       --frameworks soc2_tsc,nist_800_53,iso_27001,pci_dss_4_0

Things SOC 2 needs that digger can't provide on its own

These are deliberate scope choices. Pair digger with:

  • Identity provider audit logs (Okta, Entra ID, JumpCloud) — for CC6.2 / CC6.3 evidence of authorization and provisioning workflow.
  • HR system — for CC1.4 / CC1.5 personnel evidence.
  • Change management system (Jira, Linear, ServiceNow) — for CC8.1 change-authorization evidence.
  • Vulnerability management for application-layer CVEs that aren't in the endpoint's installed-software inventory.
  • Backup / DR exercise records — for A1.3.
  • Penetration test reports — for the auditor's risk assessment review under CC3.
  • Privacy impact assessments — for P1-P8.
